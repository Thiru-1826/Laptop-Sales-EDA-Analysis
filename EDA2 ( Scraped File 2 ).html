Business Problem¶

Lucky wants to buy a laptop in budget.¶

The online laptop market is highly competitive, with hundreds of models available across different brands, price ranges, and configurations. Customers make purchase decisions based on factors such as price, brand reputation, specifications (RAM, storage, processor), ratings, and reviews.

Lucky starts asking questions:¶

  • How do specifications (RAM, SSD, processor) impact laptop pricing?

  • Is there a relationship between price and customer ratings/reviews?

  • Is there a relationship between price and customer ratings/reviews?

  • What brand has high rating ?

  • Which laptop brands are available the most?

  • Do laptops with more RAM usually cost more?

Business Problem Statement¶

To analyze the laptop market by web scraping e-commerce data in order to identify pricing trends, brand performance, and key factors influencing customer purchase decisions.

So, we will use Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to:

Compare prices across different brands

Analyze the relationship between ratings and price

Identify features that increase laptops prices

EDA Goals¶

  • Analyze the distribution of laptop prices in the online market

  • Compare laptop prices across different brands

  • Identify brands with the highest number of laptop listings

  • Study how customer ratings vary across brands

  • Analyze the relationship between laptop price and customer ratings

CONCLUSION¶

  • RAM has a strong impact on laptop price.¶

    Laptops with higher RAM configurations tend to be priced significantly higher, indicating that memory capacity is an important cost driver for consumers and manufacturers.

  • Customer rating does not significantly influence price.¶

    Although higher-rated laptops reflect better user satisfaction, ratings alone do not lead to an increase in price. Pricing appears to be driven more by hardware specifications than by customer feedback.

  • CPU type significantly affects laptop price.¶

    Different processors show clear price variations, with higher-performance CPUs generally associated with higher-priced laptops. This confirms that CPU is a major determinant of laptop pricing.

Overall Insight¶

  • Laptop prices are primarily influenced by core hardware specifications (RAM and CPU) rather than user ratings. This suggests that consumers pay more for performance and capability, while ratings serve more as a trust or quality indicator rather than a pricing factor.
